PowerBite Ingredients

The PowerBite oral health supplement consists of plant extracts and important minerals that support the health and well-being of the teeth. Each ingredient has been tested for its purity and is also found to benefit teeth and gum health.

The PowerBite ingredients are listed below in detail. 

1

Calcium Carbonate:

Calcium carbonate is a compound that contains calcium, carbon, and oxygen. In this formula, the powder form of this compound is used which acts as a pH regulator. When the candy is dissolved in the mouth, Calcium carbonate helps to remove the plaque buildup on the teeth surface. It acts as a slight abrasive and removes stains from teeth.
2

Xylitol:

Xylitol is a natural sugar that is plant-based and used in many candies, mouth rinsers, and toothpaste as a low-calorie substitute for sugar. It reduces tooth decay and also lowers the risk of cancer. 
3

Lysozyme: 

Lysozyme is a protein in the form of an antimicrobial enzyme commonly found in plants as well as animal tissues. It shields the mouth and teeth from harmful microbial activity. It helps maintain a healthy oral microbiome.
4

Mediterranean Sea Salt: 

It is a mineral salt specially harvested from the water collected from the Mediterranean Sea. Unlike regular salt, Mediterranean sea salt contains all the natural elements as they are not filtered out. This PowerBite ingredient prevents infections and supports oral health.
5

Myrrh:

Myrrh is a fragrant resin that is obtained from particular trees and has medicinal as well as aromatic properties. It is used in mouthwashes and helps to remove plaque build-up from teeth.  
6

Wild Mint:

Wild mint is a herb in the mint family that is usually seen in marshes and near ponds and rivers. It helps to repair decayed teeth. The refreshing properties of mint provide a fresh breath.
7

Clove Oil:

Clove oil is an essential oil obtained from the dried buds of clove, often used as a spice. It relieves toothache and has antioxidant and anti-inflammation properties. It is used in toothpaste due to its cavity-fighting properties.
